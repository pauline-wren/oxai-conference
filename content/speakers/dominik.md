Dominik Lukeš is an AI consultant at the University of Oxford's AI Competency Centre (Oxford e-Research Centre). He designs and delivers training on generative AI for researchers across the University. His background is in cognitive linguistics and he especially focuses on how Large Language Models relate to human cognition, language, and learning.


## Talk Abstract

The agentic revolution of 2026 has turned the chatbot into a relic. As frontier models cross the reliability threshold from sometimes useful to near always reliable, the real innovation isn't in conversation, but in the paradoxical return to the UNIX model of "files in folders." This talk will explore a new world of research and study that opens up now that agents have made it possible to return to the resilience of plain text that we can control and easily manage. It will show how rescuing our data from chats and other proprietary formats can not only give us full ownership of our digital lives again but also allow us to do better research and deeper thinking.
