Josh Lawman is the Founder of Adder.


## Talk Abstract

It's tempting when we first use AI coding tools to approach our work in a single thread - one conversation, one context, one agent collaborating with us through a task from start to finish. That approach, while effective, introduces arbitrary limitations in world where agents and sub-agents aren't a scarce resource. However, it is difficult to break out of the mental model and come up with effective approaches to our work that leverage more than one agent. 

This talk introduces subagents and agent teams in coding CLIs, and the intuitions behind using them well. Many coding CLIs are automatically delegating your work to sub-agents, but it can be difficult to build intuitions into why they exist and how you can better leverage them. As someone trying to use more than one agent, you have an almost unlimited number of approaches to take - should I use 4 parallel agents, 1 agent team, or one agent with 3 sub agents? 

You'll be shown examples of various ways agents collaborate and walk away with new intuitions for structuring your agentic work.
