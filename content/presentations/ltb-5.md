title: "Energy-Adaptive Distributed AI Infrastructure: Integrating Compute, Energy Markets, and Governance"
---

The rapid expansion of artificial intelligence is transforming computation into a large-scale industrial energy load. Yet most AI infrastructure remains geographically static, operating within environments characterized by volatile energy prices, heterogeneous GPU performance, and growing regulatory constraints. At the same time, large data centers increasingly place pressure on local energy systems and communities, leading to rising social and environmental concerns. This talk introduces KEHAI, a framework for energy-adaptive distributed AI infrastructure that dynamically orchestrates workloads across regions based on energy prices, carbon intensity, hardware health conditions, and latency constraints. By combining GPU health monitoring, energy-aware scheduling, and inference traceability, the system explores how cross-regional compute resource sharing could enable more sustainable and socially responsible AI deployment.
