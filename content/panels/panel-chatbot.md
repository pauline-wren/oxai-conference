Artificial intelligence has given rise to something both novel and uncanny in its nature: the AI chatbot. While conversational systems have existed for decades, recent advances in transformer-based architectures have endowed them with a new social salience. For the first time, machines can speak in ways that are, at least prima facie, indistinguishable from human conversation.

What does such a phenomenon demand of us? How should we understand it, and what questions does it raise for our societies and our selves?

Join Narankar Sehmi, Evelyn Schneider, Rob Bowker, Dominik Lukeš, and Austin Andrews for a live podcast exploring the emerging world of AI chatbots. Together, they will examine the philosophical, ethical, social, and psychological implications of conversational AI: whether machines can truly “care,” whether they might deserve moral consideration, and what it means for humans to form relationships with entities that only simulate understanding.

 

## Topics

 

- Can AI Care?

- Why do AI Chatbots pose intriguing and important questions?

- What are the roles of different disciplines in approaching this new era of chatbots?

- Is it Moral to Date your AI?
